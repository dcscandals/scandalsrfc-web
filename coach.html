Washington Scandals RFC

Coach the Scandals

A growing club now going five years strong, the Scandals are seeking a new head coach to start in the Spring 2019 season. Over the first five years, the Scandals have grown immensely in size, talent, and prominence within the community. As a proud member of International Gay Rugby (IGR), the Scandals have competed on the world stage in two Bingham Cup Tournaments since 2016. At the 2018 Cup in Amsterdam, the Scandals were awarded the Ben Cohen Stand Up Award for Team Sportsmanship in recognition of their positive attitude and respect for other players. Locally, the Scandals are a member of Division IV of the Mid-Atlantic Conference. Despite the more transient nature of the National Capital Region, the Scandals membership has been steadily growing, with strong potential to develop a second side in the coming year.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Foster development among all players, with experience levels ranging from no experience to multiple years
  • Develop practice plans incorporating the captain(s) and assistant coach(es)
  • Lead practices two nights per week
  • Coach all home and away matches
  • Ensure the safety of all players
  • Promote sportsmanlike conduct and a positive attitude

Qualifications

Required

  • Minimum of 5 years combined experience as a rugby player and/or coach
  • Ability to obtain 200-level USA Rugby coaching certification within 6 months of hiring
  • Strong communication and leadership skills

Desired

  • Multiple years experience coaching rugby
  • 300-level USA Rugby coaching certification

Compensation

  • Negotiable annual stipend
  • Free lodging for all tournaments/matches requiring overnight stays
  • Free logding and travel for Bingham Cup 2020 (Ottawa)
  • Full club membership
  • Assistance with costs of USA Rugby certification

Location

All practices and home matches take place within the District of Columbia. Exact location varies by season based on allocation. Away matches in the Mid Atlantic Conference are typically within a 2 hour drive, tournaments and friendlies can be farther away, most located along the East Coast.
